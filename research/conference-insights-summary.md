## Nine Cross-Cutting Themes

These themes, extracted from the full-day conference, form the intellectual backbone of the workshop curriculum.

### T1 — Fix the Work, Not the Tools
**Workshop module:** Module 2 (Workforce Architecture)
- Nico Decock's core argument: planning counts heads, architecture designs the system
- The workforce is no longer human-only: humans + agents + digital twins + decision systems
- ~90% say job descriptions do not reflect actual work; 30-50% of tasks redundant
- Reframe: "That's not an AI strategy. That's intelligence architecture."

### T2 — Multiplication, Not Addition (AI x Human)
**Workshop module:** Module 1 (Landscape), Module 7 (Tools)
- Remko Verheul/Wrike: AI x Human Intelligence, where AI x 0 = 0
- DNEG: "All companies will have the same tools — train on craft, not tool"
- The trained eye/judgement is the moat

### T3 — Adoption Is Culture/Trust, Not Tech
**Workshop module:** Module 3 (Adoption, Culture and Trust)
- Fire triangle: people = fuel, culture = oxygen, leadership = spark
- Culture = what you celebrate, what you tolerate, what you demand
- Vattenfall "seasons" model: pull, not push
- KAIRO grassroots: 0 to 400 from one post, no budget
- The productivity-honesty trap

### T4 — Governance as Enabler, Not Control Tower
**Workshop module:** Module 4 (Governance)
- Brakes analogy (Microsoft): brakes let you go fast safely
- Personal-productivity vs enterprise AI as the one decision rule (NTT Data)
- AI Council run by the people: elected, cross-functional, solutions-oriented
- Two-speed process: 95% fast, 5% deep

### T5 — ROI Is the Battleground
**Workshop module:** Module 5 (Business Case)
- Value = (Speed x Quality x Scale) / Cost (Teradata)
- Measure outcomes, not activity/licences/usage
- Worked example: 50 licences x £360 = £18k → £324k value (18x ROI)
- 70/20/10: People & Process / Tech & Data / Algorithm (BCG)
- Only ~26% of AI projects pass POC

### T6 — The Agentic Shift
**Workshop module:** Module 6 (Agentic AI)
- HR moves from systems of record to systems that do the work (Workday)
- Team of HR Agents under an orchestration layer
- Agent sprawl: uncontrolled proliferation creates hidden estate
- HR's new job: teach the org to be a good "agent boss"

### T7 — The Job-Losses Honesty Gap
**Workshop module:** Module 9 (Honesty Conversation)
- Most speakers say "no job losses" — likely means smaller teams, different roles
- The productivity-honesty trap: if people fear, they hide gains
- Walmart's "no firings" pledge as a trust device
- WEF: -92M / +170M / +78M net — net positive but requiring massive redistribution

### T8 — Human Skills and Cognitive Risk
**Workshop module:** Module 1 (Landscape), Module 9 (Honesty Conversation)
- 47-sec screen focus vs 2.5 min in 2004 (Gloria Mark, UC Irvine)
- The Google effect (Sparrow et al., Science 2011)
- 72% less effort with more AI trust (Microsoft & CMU 2025)
- Cognitive debt: AI-assisted writers engage brains least (MIT Media Lab 2025)
- Critical thinking, communication, problem-solving, judgement/accountability

### T9 — HR From Police to Driver
**Workshop module:** Module 8 (Councils/Champions), Module 10 (90-Day Plan)
- "Drive the change, or you'll get driven — and you may not like where" (NTT Data)
- Needs courage: knowing when to tell the CEO "no, not for two years"
- Cross-functional partnership: non-HR AI person on the team; partner with CIO/CTO
