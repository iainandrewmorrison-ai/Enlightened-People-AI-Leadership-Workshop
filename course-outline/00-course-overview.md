# Strategic AI Leadership for HR — Course Overview

## Programme Philosophy

**"Fix the work, not the tools."**

Most AI training teaches people how to use tools. Most AI conferences teach people what to think about AI. Neither teaches HR leaders how to lead AI adoption in a people function — how to redesign work, build trust, govern responsibly, measure ROI, and have the honest conversations about what changes.

This 2-day workshop fills that gap. It is built on three principles:

1. **AI multiplies — it does not replace.** AI x Human Intelligence. AI x 0 = 0. The quality of the human input determines the quality of the AI output. (Wrike/Remko Verheul)
2. **Adoption is a culture problem, not a technology problem.** The technology works. The question is whether people trust it, leaders model it, and the organisation's culture rewards using it. (Vattenfall, Imperial Brands, 2 Sisters)
3. **HR leaders must drive this, or be driven.** The function that owns people strategy, organisational design, learning, and talent is the function that should own how AI reshapes all four. If HR does not lead, IT will — and they will optimise for efficiency, not people. (NTT Data)

## Programme Design

### Format
- **2 full days, in-person** (can be adapted to 3 half-days virtual)
- **12-20 participants** — senior HR professionals from the same organisation or an open cohort
- **No laptops required for Day 1** (strategy and frameworks)
- **Laptops required for Day 2** (practical application and planning)

### What Participants Leave With
1. A personal **AI leadership self-assessment** scored against the 5-level fluency framework
2. A **workforce architecture audit** of one function or process
3. A draft **AI governance framework** (one-page, not a policy binder)
4. An **ROI model** they can present to their board
5. A **90-day AI leadership action plan** with named owners and milestones

### Pre-Work (Sent 1 Week Before)
- Complete the AI Leadership Self-Assessment (15 min)
- Read: "The State of AI in HR 2026" briefing note (2 pages, provided)
- Bring: One process or function they want AI to improve

## Day 1: Strategic AI Leadership

### Module 1 — The AI Landscape for HR Leaders (75 min)

**Purpose:** Level-set the room. Most HR leaders have heard a lot about AI but struggle to separate signal from noise. This module gives them the facts, the trends, and the framework to think clearly.

**Key Content:**
- Where AI actually is in June 2026 (not where vendors say it is)
- The numbers that matter: adoption rates, ROI reality, the job market shift
  - 87% use AI weekly; 85% save 1-7 hrs/wk; but 37% of saved time lost to rework (Workday, 3,200 FTEs)
  - -92M jobs displaced, +170M new, +78M net (WEF Future of Jobs 2025)
  - 80% of AI transformations fail to scale (MIT/Bain/BCG)
  - AI capability advances every ~4 months; frontier models double every ~5.2 months
- Three things AI can be: chatbot, automation, agent (Arnold Clark framework)
- The cognitive risk: 47-second screen focus, the Google effect, 72% less effort with more AI trust (Gloria Mark, Sparrow et al., Microsoft & CMU)
- "AI is the new business English" — baseline literacy, not a specialism

**Exercise:** *The AI reality check* — participants score their organisation on 10 adoption indicators and compare with the room.

---

### Module 2 — Workforce Architecture, Not Just Workforce Planning (90 min)

**Purpose:** The biggest strategic idea of the day. Most organisations are doing workforce planning (counting heads). Almost none are doing workforce architecture (designing who does what — human, machine, or both — and who decides).

**Key Content:**
- Planning vs architecture: "Planning counts heads. Architecture designs the whole system — human work, machine work, who decides, who's on the hook." (Nico Decock)
- The workforce is no longer human-only: humans + agents + digital twins + decision systems
- ~90% say their job description does not reflect their actual work; 30-50% of tasks are redundant
- The five-level AI fluency model (adapted from Wrike):
  - L1 Practitioner — personal speed-up
  - L2 Co-Pilot — context-feeding, templates, documented prompt libraries
  - L3 Builder — shared integrations and templates
  - L4 Skilled — agents, automations, evaluation and monitoring
  - L5 Orchestrator — multi-agent orchestration, governance, audit trail
- Nico Decock's measurement shifts:
  - How much → How good
  - How busy → How smart the call
  - What one person cranked out → Who they lifted
  - The one question for every review: "What did this person do that the machine couldn't?"

**Exercise:** *The architecture audit* — take the process/function each participant brought and map it: what is human work, what is machine work, what is the decision layer, what is redundant?

### Module 3 — Adoption, Culture and the Trust Equation (75 min)

**Purpose:** Technology is not the blocker. Culture is. This module gives HR leaders the frameworks to diagnose their organisation's AI culture and the levers to shift it.

**Key Content:**
- Why 80% fail: the fire triangle (Remko Verheul/Wrike)
  - People = fuel
  - Culture = oxygen
  - Leadership = spark
  - "You can't delegate the spark" — Lead the Charge / Show Up Real / Build the Bridge
- Culture is three things: what you celebrate, what you tolerate, what you demand
  - "Culture is what people do when no one's watching" (Tony Cooke)
- Why teams don't adopt: 44% no time, 22% don't trust output, 19% don't know where to start
- The trust equation: Trust = Competence + Character (Stephen M.R. Covey, The Speed of Trust)
- Pull, not push: Vattenfall's "seasons" model — learning as Netflix series, not mandates
- The KAIRO grassroots model (Imperial Brands/Clarissa Schuhmacher): 0 to 400 of 1,000 staff, from one internal post, no budget
- The productivity-honesty trap: if people fear saved time means "more work or fewer of us," they hide the gains. Fix: be explicit about what the saved time is for
- 2.5x more motivated to embrace AI when job security feels increasing, not threatened (Edelman 2025)
- The licence catch-22: "need the case to get the licence; need the licence to build the case"

**Exercise:** *The culture diagnostic* — score your organisation on the fire triangle. Where is the spark missing? Where is the oxygen thin? What is being tolerated that should not be?

---

### Module 4 — Governance as an Enabler (60 min)

**Purpose:** Governance is the word that kills energy in a room. This module reframes it. Governance is brakes — and brakes let you go fast safely.

**Key Content:**
- The brakes analogy (Ha Cole/Microsoft): brakes let you go fast safely. No brakes = no speed.
- Governance by police vs governance as enabler:
  - "Governance by police" → talent and value leak out
  - Agile governance → few risks up front, policies reviewed monthly, a working group that solves rather than admires problems
- The one decision rule: personal-productivity AI vs enterprise AI (Akhilesh Nair/NTT Data)
  - One guideline everyone can follow instead of a policy nobody reads
- The two-speed process: 95% fast (personal productivity tools), 5% deep (enterprise-grade, data-heavy)
- The AI Council run by the people (NTT Data):
  - Elected, cross-functional
  - Runs its own hackathons
  - Owns use-case prioritisation
  - Not a sign-off committee — a solutions committee
- Lawless vs lawful agents (Workday): the agent that hires a contractor with no background check — why guardrails are non-negotiable
- Shadow AI: chatbots "popping up in Singapore, Italy, Spain" — value created but not captured
- Data quality and the "single source of truth" challenge
- EU AI Act positioning and GDPR intersections

**Exercise:** *The one-page governance framework* — draft your organisation's AI governance approach using the personal/enterprise split, the AI Council model, and the monthly review cycle.

### Module 5 — Building the Business Case: ROI That Boards Believe (75 min)

**Purpose:** "Most organisations have AI; most can't tell you what it's worth." This module gives HR leaders the tools to measure, prove, and communicate the value of AI — in language boards trust.

**Key Content:**
- The ROI battleground: why it matters now
  - IDC $227B global AI spend 2025; only ~26% pass POC (BCG, Oct 2024)
  - 70/20/10 rule: People & Process / Tech & Data / Algorithm (BCG)
- Teradata's value equation: Value = (Speed x Quality x Scale) / Cost
- Measure outcomes, not activity/licences/usage
- The worked example: 50 Copilot licences x £360 = £18k → ~1.5 days/week saved → ~£324k value (~18x ROI) (Teradata)
- Before/after comparisons and storytelling with data
- Top Performing AI Organisations (Workday): measure outcomes, reinvest savings into people's skills, update job design
- Wrike's real adoption data:
  - Tier 1 (5+/day): +25% productivity, 85 people
  - Tier 2 (1-5/day): +15%, 52 people
  - Tier 3 (2-4/wk): +0%, 60 people
  - Tier 4 (<1/wk): 0%, 32 people
  - Weighted average: +14%
  - Engineering aimed for 30%, stalled at 14%, restructured, now climbing toward 25%
- Anatomy of a Failed Pilot (Visier): 89% fear factor, only ~1/3 funded for reskilling, Shadow AI resistance
- MIT contact centre study (5,000 agents): +15% overall, 0% tenured, +34% new hires

**Exercise:** *The board slide* — build a one-page ROI case for AI in your function using the value equation, real before/after data, and the storytelling framework. Each participant presents to the room for peer challenge.

## Day 2: From Strategy to Action

### Module 6 — The Agentic Shift: What Is Coming and What HR Must Do (60 min)

**Purpose:** Agents are the next wave. Most HR leaders have heard the word but do not understand what it means for their function, their teams, or their operating model. This module prepares them.

**Key Content:**
- What an agent is: goal + tools + steps (not just a chatbot, not just an automation)
- The Team of HR Agents (Workday model):
  - Talent Acquisition, Workforce Mgmt, Talent Mgmt, HR Service, Payroll, Total Rewards, Learning + Self-Service agents under an orchestration layer
  - Agent lifecycle: Understand → Think → Act → Learn
- Agent sprawl: the hidden risk of uncontrolled proliferation
- HR's new job: teach the organisation to be a good "agent boss"
- Agent task success: 16% → 66% (Teradata) — the improvement curve is steep
- The semi-autonomous risk: an agent that deletes the codebase (Ha Cole/Microsoft)
- Bounded by permissions: agents only see what you can see
- From systems of record to systems that do the work

**Exercise:** *The agent map* — identify 3 processes in your HR function that could become agent-assisted. For each: what is the goal, what tools does it need, what guardrails are non-negotiable, who is the human in the loop?

### Module 7 — AI Tools for HR Leaders: Enough to Be Credible and Dangerous (90 min)

**Purpose:** HR leaders do not need to be power users. They need to be credible — to know what the tools can do, to recognise good work from bad, and to challenge their teams effectively. This module gives them enough hands-on experience to lead with confidence.

**Key Content:**
- The 10:80:10 rule: you bring the first 10% (brief and context), AI does the 80% (draft), you own the last 10% (check, judgement, sign-off)
- CLARITY prompting framework:
  - C = Context, L = Limits, A = Audience, R = Role, I = Inputs, T = Task, Y = Yield
- Live demonstrations (facilitator-led, then participant practice):
  - Strategic synthesis: turning a data tracker into a board-ready summary
  - Meeting intelligence: recapping decisions, owners, and actions from a Teams meeting
  - Deep research: structured briefings on HR policy questions
  - Email at scale: drafting consistent, professional communications in your voice
- Bad prompt vs good prompt comparison (Arnold Clark model)
- Bias and hallucination: practical strategies, not theory
  - "Ask it to cite each point. No citation, no reliance."
  - Confidence levels, assumption flagging, reasoning transparency
- Token cost literacy: "Claude is bloody expensive again" — understanding what things cost and why it matters
- Building a prompt library: the habit that compounds

**Exercise:** *The leader's challenge* — each participant runs a real task from their pre-work through the tools, gets a result, and presents it. The room critiques: is the output trustworthy? Would you sign off on this?

### Module 8 — Building Your AI Organisation: Councils, Champions and Communities (75 min)

**Purpose:** Individual AI literacy is not enough. Organisations need structures — councils, champions, communities — to sustain adoption beyond the initial enthusiasm. This module gives HR leaders the blueprints.

**Key Content:**
- The AI Council model (NTT Data):
  - Elected, cross-functional, not appointed by management
  - Runs hackathons and owns use-case prioritisation
  - One simple guardrail: personal-productivity vs enterprise AI
  - Solutions committee, not sign-off committee
- The champions/grassroots model (Imperial Brands KAIRO):
  - Start with one post, grow organically
  - 0 → 85 → ~400 of 1,000 staff
  - No budget required
  - The licence catch-22 and how to navigate it
- The seasons model (Vattenfall):
  - Learning as Netflix series — serialised, desirable, with a next-episode hook
  - Pull, not push: mandate → compliance, not adoption
  - 95% Copilot adoption across 7,500 licences at 21,000-employee org
- The AI Centre of Excellence (Microsoft model):
  - "Identify the weirdos" — find and empower your early adopters
  - Cross-functional partnership: a non-HR AI person on the team
  - Partner with the CIO/CTO, do not compete
- The AI-agent build competition (Wrike):
  - Certify only when someone deploys a real agent
  - Gamification: Matrix-themed Slack avatars, "secret agents" channel
  - Competition drives adoption harder than mandates

**Exercise:** *Your adoption blueprint* — design your organisation's AI adoption structure: council composition, champions identification, learning cadence, success metrics, and first 3 actions.

### Module 9 — The Honesty Conversation: Jobs, Skills and What Changes (60 min)

**Purpose:** The elephant in every room. Will AI mean job losses? Most speakers say no. The truth is more nuanced — and HR leaders need to be able to have this conversation honestly. Ducking it destroys trust.

**Key Content:**
- The macro picture: -92M displaced, +170M new, +78M net (WEF) — net positive, but "requiring massive structural redistribution"
- What "no job losses" actually means: smaller teams, radically different roles, higher expectations
- The productivity-honesty trap: if people fear saved time means "more work or fewer of us," they hide the gains
- Walmart's "no firings" pledge as a trust device
- The US non-profit model: transparent, budget-driven layoffs framed as "reset to be future-ready"
- Be explicit about what the saved time is for
- The cognitive risk conversation:
  - 47-second screen focus (down from 2.5 min in 2004)
  - 72% less effort the more we trust AI
  - Cognitive debt: AI-assisted writers engage their brains least (MIT Media Lab)
  - "This is the cognitive state we hand AI; it amplifies it"
- The human skills that matter: critical thinking, communication, problem-solving, planning, judgement/accountability
  - "AI will never take accountability"
  - "All companies will have the same tools — train them on the fundamentals of their craft, not on the tool" (DNEG)
- "Drawing without drawing" — why the trained eye and judgement is the moat
- EY data: 15,000 employees, only 5% use AI at a high level, 88% use it somehow, only 28% reach transformational results

**Exercise:** *The team conversation script* — draft the honest conversation you need to have with your team about AI and jobs. What will you say? What questions will you pre-empt? What commitments will you make?

### Module 10 — Your 90-Day AI Leadership Plan (90 min)

**Purpose:** The workshop only works if something changes on Monday. This module turns two days of learning into a concrete, accountable plan.

**Key Content:**
- The intelligence architecture question: "That's not an AI strategy. That's intelligence architecture — who makes it, who checks it, who owns it, who answers when it acts." (Nico Decock)
- The reframe: "What is work even for? And what are people even for?"
- Three measurement shifts to implement immediately:
  - How much → How good
  - How busy → How smart the call
  - What one person cranked out → Who they lifted
- The 90-day framework:
  - **Days 1-30:** Foundations — AI self-assessment for your team, governance one-pager, 3 use cases identified
  - **Days 31-60:** Build — AI Council formed, champions identified, first use cases live, ROI baseline set
  - **Days 61-90:** Scale — Learning seasons launched, cross-functional partnerships, board report delivered, second wave of use cases scoped
- Peer accountability: plans shared and challenged in small groups
- The commitment: name it, date it, own it

**Exercise:** *The 90-day plan* — complete the structured template with specific actions, owners, dates, and success measures. Present to a peer pair for challenge and commitment.
